About the Event

The Climate Action Hackathon Malaysia brings students, developers, designers and community innovators together to co-create digital tools that reduce urban heat and increase flood resilience. Participants will form teams, access curated datasets and attend hands-on workshops to build prototypes, visualizations or community apps.

Purpose

Create practical, community-impacting digital products that transform climate data into solutions.

Goals

  • Prototype at least 10 working solutions (dashboards, apps, alerts)
  • Integrate Malaysian datasets & community feedback
  • Promote SDG 13 — Climate Action

Expected Outcomes

Datasets cleaned and documented, early prototypes, demo-ready community tools, technical briefs.

Connection to SDG 13 — Climate Action

Solutions from the hackathon are intended to reduce climate risks by improving community awareness, enabling early-warning systems for floods and mapping/mitigating urban heat using geospatial tools and green infrastructure planning.

Heat mitigation

Map heat hotspots, propose shading and tree-planting priorities and prototype cooling strategies.

Flood resilience

Build early-warning dashboards, local evacuation plans and water-level visualisations.

Capacity building

Workshops and mentoring to ensure solutions are inclusive and maintainable.
